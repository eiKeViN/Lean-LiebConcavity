3 Supporting Results

This chapter collects auxiliary results used in later chapters. The first is a spectral containment lemma for conjugate-weighted sums. The second is a commutativity identity for real powers in a C\(^*\)-algebra. The third section develops the Fourier averaging machinery used in the proof of Jensen’s operator inequality. The fourth records an inner product monotonicity lemma used in the Lieb and Ando results.

3.1 Conjugate-Weighted Sums

Lemma 13 Spectrum of a conjugate-weighted sum
✓
#

Lean declarations

  • spectrum_sum_conj_subset

Let \(\mathcal{B}\) be an ordered unital C\(^*\)-algebra, let \(I \subseteq \mathbb {R}\) be a convex subset, and let \(n \in \mathbb {N}\). Suppose \(a_1, \dots , a_n \in \mathcal{B}\) are self-adjoint with \(\sigma (a_i) \subseteq I\) for each \(i\), and let \(b_1, \dots , b_n \in \mathcal{B}\) satisfy \(\sum _i b_i^* b_i = 1\). Then

\[ \sigma \! \left(\sum _i b_i^*\, a_i\, b_i\right) \subseteq I. \]
Proof ▶

Set \(\alpha = \inf _i \inf \sigma (a_i)\) and \(\beta = \sup _i \sup \sigma (a_i)\); then \([\alpha , \beta ] \subseteq I\) by convexity and ord-connectedness. The unit constraint \(\sum _i b_i^* b_i = 1\) gives \(\alpha \cdot 1 \leq \sum _i b_i^* a_i b_i \leq \beta \cdot 1\) in the order on \(\mathcal{B}\), since each summand satisfies \(\alpha \cdot b_i^* b_i \leq b_i^* a_i b_i \leq \beta \cdot b_i^* b_i\). The order bounds imply the spectrum is contained in \([\alpha , \beta ]\), and hence in \(I\).

3.2 Real Powers in C\(^*\)-Algebras

Lemma 14 Power of a product of commuting positives
#

Lean declarations

  • CFC.mul_rpow_of_commute

Let \(\mathcal{B}\) be an ordered unital C\(^*\)-algebra, let \(a, b \in \mathcal{B}\) with \(0 \leq a\), \(0 \leq b\), and \(ab = ba\). Then for all \(r \in \mathbb {R}\),

\[ (ab)^r = a^r\, b^r, \]

where the powers are taken via the continuous functional calculus.

Proof ▶

Assumed without proof. This identity would follow from the fact that the continuous functional calculus distributes over multiplication for commuting elements: \(f(ab) = f(a)\, f(b)\) whenever \(a\) and \(b\) commute and \(f(t) = t^r\). This CFC multiplicativity for commuting pairs is not yet available in Mathlib, so the lemma is left unproved pending Mathlib support.

3.3 Fourier Averaging

Given a primitive \(m\)-th root of unity \(\zeta \in \mathbb {C}\) (with \(m \neq 0\)), the Fourier diagonal matrices \(V_k = \operatorname {diag}(\zeta ^{ki})_{i=0}^{m-1}\) for \(k = 0, \ldots , m-1\) are unitaries, and their conjugate average extracts the diagonal of any matrix.

  • geom_sum_primitive_root

Let \(\zeta \) be a primitive \(m\)-th root of unity. For any \(d \in \mathbb {Z}\),

\[ \sum _{k=0}^{m-1} \zeta ^{k\cdot d} \; =\; \begin{cases} m & \text{if } m \mid d, \\ 0 & \text{otherwise.} \end{cases} \]
Proof ▶

Standard geometric series argument: if \(m \nmid d\) then \(\zeta ^d \neq 1\), and the sum equals \((\zeta ^{md} - 1)/(\zeta ^d - 1) = 0\).

  • FourierAvg_eq_diagonal

Let \(\zeta \) be a primitive \(m\)-th root of unity, \(m \neq 0\), and \(M \in M_m(A)\). Then

\[ \frac{1}{m} \sum _{k=0}^{m-1} V_k^*\, M\, V_k \; =\; \operatorname {diagonal}(\operatorname {diag}(M)). \]
Proof ▶

The \((i,j)\)-entry of \(\sum _k V_k^* M V_k\) equals \(M_{ij} \cdot \sum _k \zeta ^{k(j-i)}\), which by 17 is \(m \cdot M_{ij}\) if \(i = j\) and \(0\) otherwise. Dividing by \(m\) gives \(\operatorname {diagonal}(\operatorname {diag}(M))\). The identification of the index set \(\{ 0,\ldots ,n\} \sqcup \{ *\} \) used in Jensen’s proof with \(\{ 0,\ldots ,n+1\} \) goes via 5.

3.4 Inner Product Monotonicity

Lemma 19 Monotonicity of the real inner product functional
✓
#

Lean declarations

  • reApplyInnerSelf_mono_right

Let \(E\) be a Hilbert space over \(\mathbb {k}\) and \(S, T : E \to _L E\) bounded operators with \(S \leq T\). Then for all \(x \in E\),

\[ \operatorname {re}\langle x,\, S x\rangle \; \leq \; \operatorname {re}\langle x,\, T x\rangle . \]
Proof ▶

\(T - S \geq 0\) implies \(\operatorname {re}\langle x, (T-S)x\rangle \geq 0\) by positivity of the sesquilinear form, and linearity gives the result.
