8 Matrix Specializations

The algebra \(M_n(\mathbb {C})\) of \(n \times n\) complex matrices, equipped with the Frobenius inner product \(\langle A, B \rangle = \operatorname {tr}(A^* B)\), is the canonical example of an H\(^*\)-algebra (Definition 9). Under this identification, the inner product functional \(K \mapsto \operatorname {re}\langle K, T(K) \rangle \) appearing in the abstract results equals \(\operatorname {re}\operatorname {tr}(T(K) \cdot K^*)\), which for the operators arising in Lieb’s and Ando’s theorems takes the explicit trace form recorded below. This chapter collects the classical matrix-theoretic statements of Lieb’s concavity theorem, its extension, and Ando’s convexity theorem as corollaries of the abstract H\(^*\)-algebra results from the preceding chapter.

[H\(^*\)-algebra instance for matrices] The algebra \(M_n(\mathbb {C})\) of \(n \times n\) complex matrices, equipped with the Frobenius inner product \(\langle A, B \rangle = \operatorname {tr}(A^* B)\), is an H\(^*\)-algebra.

Proof ▶

Direct verification of the H\(^*\)-algebra axioms using the standard Frobenius inner product and matrix multiplication.

Throughout this chapter, \(n\) is a positive integer, \(A, B \in M_n(\mathbb {C})\), \(K \in M_n(\mathbb {C})\) is arbitrary, \(A^q\) denotes the matrix power via the continuous functional calculus, \(\operatorname {PosDef}(A)\) means \(A\) is positive definite, and \(\operatorname {PosSemidef}(B)\) means \(B\) is positive semidefinite.

Let \(p, q {\gt} 0\) with \(p + q \leq 1\), and let \(K \in M_n(\mathbb {C})\). The map

\[ (A,\, B) \; \longmapsto \; \operatorname {re}\operatorname {tr}\! \bigl(A^q\, K\, B^p\, K^*\bigr) \]

is jointly concave on \(\bigl\{ (A, B) \in M_n(\mathbb {C}) \times M_n(\mathbb {C}) \mid \operatorname {PosDef}(A),\; \operatorname {PosSemidef}(B)\bigr\} \).

Proof ▶

Instantiate the abstract Lieb extension theorem (Theorem 33) at the H\(^*\)-algebra \(M_n(\mathbb {C})\) with the Frobenius inner product, and use the identification \(\operatorname {re}\langle K,\, A^q K B^p \rangle = \operatorname {re}\operatorname {tr}(A^q K B^p K^*)\) together with \(\operatorname {IsStrictlyPositive}(A) \leftrightarrow \operatorname {PosDef}(A)\) and \(0 \leq B \leftrightarrow \operatorname {PosSemidef}(B)\).

Let \(q, r \in \mathbb {R}\) with \(1 \leq q \leq 2\), \(r {\gt} 0\), and \(q - r {\gt} 1\), and let \(K \in M_n(\mathbb {C})\). The map

\[ (A,\, B) \; \longmapsto \; \operatorname {re}\operatorname {tr}\! \bigl(A^{-r}\, K\, B^q\, K^*\bigr) \]

is jointly convex on \(\bigl\{ (A, B) \in M_n(\mathbb {C}) \times M_n(\mathbb {C}) \mid \operatorname {PosDef}(A),\; \operatorname {PosSemidef}(B)\bigr\} \).

Proof ▶

Instantiate the abstract Ando convexity theorem (Theorem 35) at the H\(^*\)-algebra \(M_n(\mathbb {C})\) with the Frobenius inner product, and use the identification \(\operatorname {re}\langle K,\, A^{-r} K B^q \rangle = \operatorname {re}\operatorname {tr}(A^{-r} K B^q K^*)\) together with \(\operatorname {IsStrictlyPositive}(A) \leftrightarrow \operatorname {PosDef}(A)\) and \(0 \leq B \leftrightarrow \operatorname {PosSemidef}(B)\).
