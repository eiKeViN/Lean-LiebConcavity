1.1 OpStar Isomorphism

Proposition 1 CFC via opStar
✓
#

Lean declarations

  • opStar_map_cfc
  • op_rpow_eq_rpow_op_nonneg
  • op_rpow_eq_rpow_op_strictlyPositive

Let \(A\) be an ordered unital C\(^*\)-algebra. The opStar map \(\varphi = \operatorname {op} \circ \star : A \to A^{\mathrm{op}}\) is a star algebra isomorphism, and it intertwines the continuous functional calculus: for any continuous \(f\) and self-adjoint \(a \in A\),

\[ \varphi (f(a)) = f(\varphi (a)). \]

In particular, for \(a \geq 0\) and \(r \geq 0\), or for \(a\) strictly positive and any \(r \in \mathbb {R}\):

\[ (a^r)^{\mathrm{op}} = (a^{\mathrm{op}})^r. \]
Proof ▶

\(\varphi \) is a star algebra homomorphism, so the CFC intertwining property applies directly.

1.2 Unitary Conjugation

Theorem 2 CFC commutes with unitary conjugation
✓
#

Lean declarations

  • CStarAlgebra.cfc_unitary_conj'

Let \(A\) be a unital C\(^*\)-algebra, \(u \in A\) unitary, and \(f : \mathbb {R} \to \mathbb {R}\) continuous. For any self-adjoint \(a \in A\),

\[ f(u^* a\, u) = u^*\, f(a)\, u. \]
Proof ▶

Unitary conjugation \(x \mapsto u^* x u\) is a star algebra automorphism, so the CFC intertwining property gives \(f(u^* a u) = u^* f(a) u\).

1.3 Diagonal CFC

Theorem 3 CFC distributes over diagonal matrices
✓
#

Lean declarations

  • MatCStar.cfc_diagonal

Let \(d : \operatorname {Fin}\, n \to \mathbb {R}\) and \(f : \mathbb {R} \to \mathbb {R}\) continuous. Then

\[ f\! \bigl(\operatorname {diagonal}(d)\bigr) \; =\; \operatorname {diagonal}(f \circ d), \]

where the CFC is taken in \(M_n(\mathbb {C})\).

Proof ▶

Diagonal matrices in \(M_n(\mathbb {C})\) form a commutative C\(^*\)-subalgebra isomorphic to \(\mathbb {C}^n\), and the CFC on a diagonal matrix acts entry-wise.

Theorem 4 Order on diagonal matrices is entry-wise
✓
#

Lean declarations

  • MatCStar.diagonal_le_diagonal_iff

For \(d, e : \operatorname {Fin}\, n \to \mathbb {R}\),

\[ \operatorname {diagonal}(d) \leq \operatorname {diagonal}(e) \; \iff \; \forall i,\; d(i) \leq e(i), \]

where the order on \(M_n(\mathbb {C})\) is the Loewner (positive semidefinite) order.

Proof ▶

A diagonal matrix is positive semidefinite iff all diagonal entries are nonneg, and \(\operatorname {diagonal}(e) - \operatorname {diagonal}(d) = \operatorname {diagonal}(e - d)\), so the characterization follows.

1.4 Reindex as Star Algebra Equivalence

Proposition 5 Reindex as a star algebra equivalence
✓
#

Lean declarations

  • Matrix.reindexStarAlgEquiv

For finite types \(m \simeq n\) and a star algebra \(A\), the map \(\operatorname {reindex}\, e\, e : M_m(A) \to M_n(A)\) induced by a bijection \(e : m \simeq n\) is a star algebra equivalence.

Proof ▶

Direct verification: reindexing preserves ring operations, star, and scalar action.
