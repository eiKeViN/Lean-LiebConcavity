4 Löwner’s Theorem

This chapter contains the two key sorry nodes of the formalization: the operator convexity and operator concavity of the power function \(t \mapsto t^r\) on \([0, \infty )\). These results are classical, going back to Löwner  [ 4 ] and developed further by Hansen–Pedersen  [ 2 ] . They underpin every subsequent result in Chapters 5–7: the joint convexity/concavity of the generalized perspective function, the power mean, and ultimately Lieb’s concavity theorem and Ando’s convexity theorem.

Both theorems are currently admitted with sorry bodies in the Lean source. Their formalization depends on CFC infrastructure for real powers that is ongoing work in Mathlib (primarily by Frédéric Dupuis), and will be filled in once the required Mathlib lemmas are available.

Theorem 11 Operator convexity of the power function
#

Uses

For \(1 \leq r \leq 2\), the function \(t \mapsto t^r\) is operator convex on \([0, \infty )\). That is, for every ordered unital C\(^*\)-algebra \(\mathcal{B}\) and every pair of self-adjoint elements \(a, b \in \mathcal{B}\) with \(\sigma (a), \sigma (b) \subseteq [0, \infty )\) and every \(\lambda \in [0, 1]\),

\[ \bigl(\lambda a + (1 - \lambda ) b\bigr)^r \; \leq \; \lambda \, a^r + (1 - \lambda )\, b^r. \]

Remark 12
#

This is a classical result. Löwner  [ 4 ] characterized operator monotone functions on \([0, \infty )\); operator convexity of \(t^r\) for \(1 \leq r \leq 2\) follows from the integral representation of operator convex functions and is treated in detail by Hansen–Pedersen  [ 2 ] . The Lean formalization is currently a sorry stub; it depends on Mathlib CFC infrastructure for real powers (\(\mathtt{NNReal.rpow}\) and the associated ordered CFC lemmas) that is actively being developed.

Theorem 13 Operator concavity of the power function

For \(0 {\lt} r \leq 1\), the function \(t \mapsto t^r\) is operator concave on \([0, \infty )\). That is, for every ordered unital C\(^*\)-algebra \(\mathcal{B}\) and every pair of self-adjoint elements \(a, b \in \mathcal{B}\) with \(\sigma (a), \sigma (b) \subseteq [0, \infty )\) and every \(\lambda \in [0, 1]\),

\[ \lambda \, a^r + (1 - \lambda )\, b^r \; \leq \; \bigl(\lambda a + (1 - \lambda ) b\bigr)^r. \]
Proof ▶

Admitted (sorry).

Remark 14
#

Operator monotonicity of \(t \mapsto t^r\) for \(0 {\lt} r \leq 1\) on \([0,\infty )\) is the central result of Löwner  [ 4 ] . Operator concavity for the same range of exponents is a direct consequence; see Hansen–Pedersen  [ 2 ] for the precise connection between operator monotonicity and operator concavity. As with Theorem 11, the Lean proof is deferred pending the requisite Mathlib CFC infrastructure for real powers.
